Before you groan, I know nobody likes a checklist article. But stick with me here, because this one is actually designed to save you money rather than give you something to feel good about ticking.

Most AI adoption failures are not technology failures. They are planning failures. Something important was not thought through before the build started and it caused problems that were expensive to fix later.

Work through this before you commit to anything.

## Before You Start

You can describe the specific problem you want AI to solve in one or two sentences. Not "improve efficiency" or "use AI better." A specific problem with a specific cost attached to it. If you cannot do this yet, spend more time on diagnosis before moving to solutions.

You know roughly what solving that problem is worth to your business. Either in time saved, revenue gained, or errors avoided. You do not need an exact number but you need a ballpark. This is what you will measure your investment against.

You have talked to the people who will actually use the system. Not just executives or owners but the people whose daily work will change. Their input on what the real problem is and what they will actually use is more valuable than most people give it credit for.

## Your Data Situation

The information the AI will need to work with exists somewhere in your business. It does not need to be perfectly organized but it needs to be accessible. Customer records, past transactions, product information, whatever is relevant to the use case.

You have a rough sense of how clean that data is. Old, duplicate, or inconsistent data produces poor AI outputs. If your data is a mess, budget time to clean it up as part of the project, not as an afterthought.

You know where that data lives and who has access to it. Surprises about data access are one of the most common causes of project delays.

## Your Tools

You have listed the software your business currently uses that touches the problem you want to solve. Most AI implementations involve connecting existing tools rather than replacing them. Knowing your current stack upfront saves time.

You have checked whether your existing tools already have AI features you are not using. A surprising number of businesses buy new AI tools when their current software has relevant functionality sitting unused. Check your settings before adding subscriptions.

## Your Team

Someone on your team is designated as the owner of this project. Not just a sponsor who approved it, but someone who will be responsible for the implementation, the training, and the ongoing maintenance.

Your team knows this is coming and understands why. Springing a new system on people who were not consulted is one of the fastest ways to ensure it does not get used. A brief conversation about what is changing and why goes a long way.

You have a rough plan for training. Even simple AI tools need some onboarding. Budget time for this. The people who will use the system need to understand not just how to use it but why it works the way it does.

## Your Success Criteria

You have defined what success looks like in measurable terms before you start. Not just "it works" but something specific. Hours saved per week, leads responded to faster, support tickets deflected, reviews requested per month.

You have a timeline for your first review. We recommend 30 days after launch as the first check-in point. By then you will have enough data to know whether the system is delivering and what needs adjustment.

You have a budget for iteration. The first version of any AI implementation is rarely the final version. Build in some room for adjustments after launch.

## If You Checked Everything

You are in a good position to move forward. The businesses that do this work upfront have significantly better outcomes than those who jump straight to building.

If you found gaps, that is useful too. Now you know what to sort out first rather than discovering it midway through a build.

Either way, if you want a second opinion on what you are planning, that is exactly what a discovery call is for.
